directions

Wilsonville is about 19 miles (30 km) South of Portland, OR and 29 miles North of Salem, OR. The wedding will be at 33140 SW Ladd Hill Road, Wilsonville, OR.

From I-5, heading south, from Portland:

  • Exit the freeway at the Wilsonville exit #283

  • Turn right onto Wilsonville Road

  • Go about 5.5 miles (8.8 km) to Ladd Hill Road

  • Turn right on to Ladd Hill Road

  • Go about one mile (1.6 km) up the hill and the house will be on the right

From I-5, heading north, from Salem:

  • Exit the freeway at the Wilsonville exit #283

  • Turn left onto Wilsonville Road

  • Go about 5.5 miles (8.8 km) to Ladd Hill Road

  • Turn right on to Ladd Hill Road

  • Go about one mile (1.6 km) up the hill and the house will be on the right

From downtown Newberg:

  • Head east on 1st St

  • Stay right and continue on to OR-219 South; the road will bend to the right

  • Turn left on Springbrook

  • Turn at the first right, which will be Wilsonville Road

  • Head about 7.5 miles down Wilsonville Road and turn left at Ladd Hill Road

  • Go about one mile (1.6 km) up the hill and the house will be on the right

The mailbox, with the house number, is on the left side of the street and the house is directly across. There will be decorations and signs to help you find the place and people to assist with parking once you get there.

NOTE: If you’re using a GPS and it directs you to turn on Edminston Road, please follow the above directions instead. Edminston intersects Ladd Hill Road at point where Ladd Hill Road makes a 90 degree angle and many people get confused and take the wrong turn when going this way.

FAQ

Transportation - Getting to Portland

PDX is the closest airport.
If reserved early, BoltBus is a quite inexpensive way to get to Portland. Other trains/bus combinations can be found through Amtrak.
Driving from Vancouver along I-5 can take anywhere from 5.5 hours to 8 hours, depending on Traffic.

Weather in Oregon - What should I wear?

Oregon is generally quite warm in late July, so dress accordingly. The wedding ceremony and reception will be outdoors on grass and gravel, so wedges or flats instead of heels are recommended.
